The World Wide Web Consortium

About

"The World Wide Web Consortium, a.k.a. W3C, is an international community comprised of member organizations, a staff, and the public work together to develop Web standards.
The W3C engages in education and outreach through developing software and serving as an open forum for discussion about the Web.

The World Wide Web Consortium was founded by Tim Berners-Lee after he left the European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN) in 1994.

It was founded at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology Laboratory for Computer Science (MIT/LCS) with support from the European Commission and the Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency, which had pioneered the ARPANET, one of the predecessors to the Internet.

The organization tries to foster compatibility and agreement among industry members in the adoption of new standards defined by the W3C."

"Incompatible versions of HTML are offered by different vendors, causing inconsistency in how web pages are displayed. The consortium tries to get all those vendors to implement a set of core principles and components which are chosen by the consortium.

The Consortium is governed by its membership. The list of members is available to the public. Members are comprised of businesses, nonprofit organizations, universities, governmental entities, and individuals.

The World Wide Web Consortium is truly a marvelous collection of information."
